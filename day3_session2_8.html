Slide 56: Limitations

Can AI Be Wrong?

The Myth

"AI always knows the absolute correct answer."

The Reality
  • ❌ Gives incorrect facts dynamically
  • ❌ Invents plausible but non-existent details
  • ❌ Sounds extremely confident even when wrong
Key Concept Hallucination

Never trust AI blindly. Always verify critical metrics, dates, and historical occurrences.

Slide 57: Ethical Practice

Responsible
Prompt Engineering

With extreme technology capability comes equal ethical obligation. Our core objective is teaching AI to act as an intelligence magnifier, not a replacement for your analytical voice.

The Golden Standard:

Generate, optimize, audit, and inspect. Treat models as your dedicated intellectual co-pilot, not an autopilot machine.

Ethics must govern your prompting techniques as we transition to professional assignments.

Ethical Decision Framework

The Traffic Light Checklist

GREEN (Good Practices)
  • Constant independent fact-checking
  • Strict preservation of target privacy
  • Apply high critical evaluations
  • Use AI as an acceleration helper
RED (Avoid Practices)
  • Inputting personal, secure user details
  • Directly submitting raw, unchecked results
  • Cheating by replacing original research
  • Generating unverified viral content

Prompt Detective – Grand Quiz Challenge

Tap cards to flip them and confirm answers. Review your understanding as a class!

Investigated 0 / 10 Completed
01

What is a prompt?

Inputs or instructions directed to an AI to generate specific responses.

02

Which technique uses examples?

Few-Shot Prompting (showing clear sample inputs/outputs).

03

What does 'R' mean in CRAFT?

Role (Persona or expert perspective/stance specified).

04

What is Chain-of-Thought?

Instructing the model to output logical, step-by-step reasoning.

05

What is a hallucination?

When AI confidently fabricates false data or references.

06

Better: A ("Explain AI") or B ("You are a teacher...")?

B! Adding specific role and audience scope improves relevance vastly.

07

Name one real-world application.

Code optimization, copy drafting, legal summaries, or curriculum design.

08

Why is context important?

It limits probabilities, guiding output relevance to your situation.

09

What is Prompt Iteration?

Refining prompts in stages based on intermediate output flaws.

10

What is one ethical rule?

Do not share sensitive, identifying user coordinates or organizational data.
