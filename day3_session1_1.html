Slide 4: Core Architecture Definition

What is Generative AI?

Imagine showing a computer 10,000 pictures of cats. How would traditional AI models handle this data differently from generative AI?

Traditional AI (Student A - The Classifier)

Analyzes and classifies input. "Is this email spam?", "Is this image a cat or dog?", "Will it rain tomorrow?" - Standard analysis.

Generative AI (Student B - The Producer)

Creates something entirely new from patterns learned. Writing essays, sketching fresh graphics, creating songs, composing source code.

Official Definition: Generative AI is a branch of artificial intelligence designed to draft or produce novel assets (text, images, media, code) using structured rules recognized from massive, pre-existing training datasets.

Slide 5: Pattern Training Foundations

How Does Generative AI Learn?

How do humans learn languages? By scanning grammar rules, reading novels, engaging in discussions, and noticing recurring vocabulary structures. AI systems learn in an analogous way.

The Secret: Predicting the Next Word

Large Language Models are master probability calculators. They look at historic contexts to compute the absolute most logical next element.

"The glowing sun rises in the ____" East (99.8%)

Core warning: AI has no sentience or direct knowledge. It understands mathematical correlations, not human values.
