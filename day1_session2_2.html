Train-Test Split

To evaluate a model fairly, we divide our data. We train the model on the Training Set (practice questions) and evaluate it on the Testing Set (final exam).

How Do We Measure Success?

In a disease detection system of 100 patients (20 diseased, 80 healthy), a simple accuracy check can be misleading. Let's inspect the metrics!

Accuracy Metric

90%
Description & Intuition:

Accuracy represents the overall proportion of correct classifications made across the entire database. Out of 100 patients, the model predicted 90 correctly.

Spam Analogy: Overall Score

Like an exam grade showing overall marks. In spam filters, it tells us what percentage of total incoming emails were sorted correctly.

Repeated Testing Mock Exams

Cross Validation

If you were preparing for a critical board exam, would you solve **only one** practice paper? Probably not! If that one paper was unusually easy or hard, it would give you a false sense of security or panic.

We solve **multiple mock exams** to get a true, reliable estimate of our knowledge. In Machine Learning, this repeated testing on different slices of data is called **Cross Validation**.

Current Phase Fold 1: Mock Exam 1

5-Fold Cross Validation Simulation

Each round, a different 20% slice acts as the "Final Exam" (TEST), while the other 80% is the "Practice Material" (TRAIN). We average all scores for the final grade!

Average Validation Score: 84%
Classroom Recap

When to Use What?

80/20

Simple Train/Test Split

Best for **huge datasets** or heavy models. Quick and computationally cheap, but susceptible to a "lucky" or "unlucky" split.

K-Fold

K-Fold Cross Validation

Best for **small-to-medium datasets** where every data point is precious. Gives maximum reliability, but takes K-times longer to train.
