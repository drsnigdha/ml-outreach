A Formal Definition

What is Machine Learning?

Machine Learning (ML) is a branch of Artificial Intelligence (AI) that enables computers to learn from data and improve their performance without being explicitly programmed for every single situation.

🧠

In Simple Terms

"Machine Learning is the science of teaching computers to learn from experience."

1. Examples (Data)

Historical information acts as experiences.

2. Generalization (Learning)

The algorithm discovers structures and patterns.

3. Prediction (Outcome)

The computer infers actions on completely new variables.

The Paradigm Shift

Traditional Programming vs. ML

How do we solve problems? Let's look at the flow of inputs, outputs, and instructions.

Old Way

Traditional Programming

Humans write rules. The computer processes data strictly using those rules to output answers.

Rules + Data Answers

Example: Marks > 40 → Pass

New Way

Machine Learning

We provide historical data and matching answers. The machine discovers the underlying rules (Model) automatically.

Data + Answers Rules (Model)

Example: Past Exam Results & Habits → Automated Evaluation Rules

Step 2: Cleansing

Scrubbing Messy Databases

Real-world logs are extremely dirty. Feeding corrupt variables directly to a model guarantees complete generalization failure (GIGO).

  • ⚠️ Incorrect Entries: Impossible outliers (e.g. Age: 250)

Pillar Classification

Classification vs. Regression

How are target labels structured? Supervised modeling splits down the center based on outcome formats: categories vs. numeric scales.

Categories

Classification

Predict discrete buckets, labels, classes, or descriptors. "Which class does this sample belong to?"

Spam vs. Inbox Cat vs. Dog Pass vs. Fail

Rule: Categorical Bins. Yes/No, Red/Blue, Class A/B.

Continuous

Regression

Predict continuous infinite numeric metrics. "How much / what specific numerical value?"

House Price ($) Temperature (°C) Total Marks

Rule: Real Infinite Numbers. Temperature, Salary, Lifespans.

Intuitive Algorithms

Decision Trees

A Decision Tree is one of the most intuitive models in AI. It processes complex datasets by asking a logical sequence of questions to narrow down predictions.

Instead of mathematical formula curves, the algorithm maps out branching structures of check-gates to reach outcome classes.
